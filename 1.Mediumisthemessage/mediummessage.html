The medium is the message (just my 2 cents) By Marshall McLuhan

The medium is the message is a wonderful read by Marshall McLuhan it is the first chapter in his Understanding media: the Extensions of man which was published in 1964.

The topics of technological determinism and social construction of technology theory have been very interesting conversations. I don’t think that there is a doubt that technology plays a role in the social and economic but whether it is the deciding factor or just a tool is interesting to read about. Construction of technology in brief states that technology is not the deciding factor but rather that we decide how we use technology, technology develops as a result of us. While technological determinism claims that technology is the main factor in the growth and progression of society. There’s more to it than that I know, but Marshal McLuhan offers an interesting analogy.

Marshal McLuhan I would say offers a soft technological determinism perspective, where he states that the medium is the message. I’ve been trying to better grasp what he means by this because the phrase in itself is unique. McLuhan does not explicitly say that the medium or technology is the ultimate determining factor in how as society progresses, however it does play and important role. The medium is neither good nor bad nor is it neutral. The media itself can shape the people who use it. He reckons that we can tell a lot about a civilization based on the mediums that they used rather than the content that was on that media or communicated through that medium.

Something many people do is watch tv whether it be the news, movies etc., and we do its unconsciously or maybe not unconsciously, but we don’t really think about what watching tv means and what it does to us and why it does that do us. How does watching tv impact us what does it do to society. What even is television? I probably just repeated the same sentence here in a different format, but I do feel like rephrasing questions are good in understanding.

If I think regarding communication in Instagram when someone sends a message via, you have the option to just like the message, that action can be thought of as communication, how has Instagram and apps alike have influenced our ideas of what communication is.

An example used in the reading or not only in the reading, but many times is the one that guns are not good nor are they bad, but the way people use them determines the meaning. This sentiment can be used for many technologies. What is the concept of a gun, what is good and what is bad?

Clothes as a form/medium of communication. I’m not going to get to much into it but it’s interesting to think about how clothing has had an impact on us. Fashion trends, beliefs, what does the fashion of the 60’s vs now say about society.

The concepts behind technological determinism and social construction of technology have my mind all over the place. The more you think, the more you think about what you thought. Personally, I wouldn’t be able to give my firm position, but I feel its important to engage with both theories and not dismiss either because both bring up good points and both have their flaws, but we can use them as artists or as non-artists (personally I feel like we are all artists) to understand our content and our mediums, what we are creating.
