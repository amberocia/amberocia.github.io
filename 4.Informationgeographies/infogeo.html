Information Geographies By Graham

“Information has always had geography. It is from somewhere; about somewhere; it evolves and is transformed somewhere, it is mediated by networks, infrastructures, and technologies: all of which exist in physical, material places. Information has also always been mobile” ( (Graham, De Sabbata and Zook, 2015)

A quick look at the word Geography and I already feel lost in translation but nonetheless Mark Graham provides some interesting insight into information geographies. If you’re like me and suck at geography fear not the term Information geographies is not as scary as it sounds:)

In an age of almost ubiquitous potential connectivity so many people are still left out of global networks and debates and conversations. So despite the rapid growth in internet connectivity and access most of the world is still experiencing a kind of disconnectedness and even with those connected many are still being left out of debates conversations etc.(Graham, De Sabbata and Zook, 2015)It is important for information to spread evenly across the global yet even though we live in an era where connectivity has become easier compared to past methods, we still see an uneven distribution, production, and use of information and or knowledge. And like graham states in his article the transmission and movement of information is an ongoing and dynamic process. (Graham, De Sabbata and Zook, 2015) Information meaning any and all information be it stories, legends, academia you name it. Its important to note that because this information contribute to how we understand and view places. Information produced both in and about a place contributes significantly because they shape how we are able to find and understand various parts of the world.

Something many people do is watch tv whether it be the news, movies etc., and we do its unconsciously or maybe not unconsciously, but we don’t really think about what watching tv means and what it does to us and why it does that do us. How does watching tv impact us what does it do to society. What even is television? I probably just repeated the same sentence here in a different format, but I do feel like rephrasing questions are good in understanding.

Graham talks about (im)mutable augmentations of information. Honestly that sounds a bit intense. Breaking each work into their own definitions (according to oxford/google) immutable means ‘unchanging over time or unable to be changed’ and mutable is the opposite, augmentation is ‘the action or process of making or becoming greater is size’ and lastly information is ‘facts provided or learned about something or someone’ or as in the article ‘the imparting of knowledge’. Put that all together coupled with some information gathered from the article (im)mutable augmentations of information from my understanding is the dynamic processes of information production and transferral that is or has been made unchangeable/changeable.
